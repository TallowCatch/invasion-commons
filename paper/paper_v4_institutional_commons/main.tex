\begin{abstract}
This draft extends the Harvest Commons line toward a more policy-interpretable institutional design study. The paper keeps the same research focus---renewable commons, strategic pressure, and governance architecture---while adding literature-backed scenario archetypes, explicit oversight frictions, and LLM-generated strategy populations. The central question is which institutional arrangements remain robust under strategic noncompliance when oversight is imperfect and the underlying policy population is no longer restricted to hand-designed or search-based strategies.
\end{abstract}
