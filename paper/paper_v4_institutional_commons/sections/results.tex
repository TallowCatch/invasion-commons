\section{Results Plan}

\subsection{Architecture Under Institutional Frictions}

The first empirical module asks whether the current Harvest architecture result survives once governance is made more realistic. The main comparison remains \texttt{hybrid} versus \texttt{top\_down\_only}, with \texttt{bottom\_up\_only} retained as the decentralized comparison case. Primary outcomes are patch health, neighborhood overharvest, welfare, garden failure, and governance burden.

\subsection{Governance Maps for LLM Strategy Populations}

The second module evaluates sampled Harvest populations drawn from LLM-generated strategy banks. Populations vary in exploitative share, and governance is compared over the same ecological and welfare outcomes. The purpose is to test whether the architecture ranking persists once the population includes model-generated policies rather than only hand-designed or search-generated strategies.

\subsection{Turnover Pilot}

The third module introduces a limited population-dynamics bridge to the recent LLM collective-behaviour literature. Agents are resampled from survivor-weighted strategy banks over generations, allowing the paper to track whether exploitative strategy families spread and whether governance can slow or prevent that shift.
