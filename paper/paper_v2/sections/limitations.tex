\section{Limitations}

The two studies answer adjacent questions, not identical ones. The first study is a controlled signal-ranking study in Fishery Commons, while the second study is a governance-architecture extension in Harvest Commons. The second study shares the same invasion loop and replacement logic as the first, which resolves the earlier asymmetry, but it still should not be read as a strict replication in another substrate because the governance question itself is broader.

The LLM comparison appears only in the Fishery Commons study as main-paper evidence. These results are sufficient to show that minimal central governance survives both mutation and live JSON-based strategy generation, but they do not establish the same result for Harvest Commons. A live Harvest LLM path was implemented and cleared an initial reliability screen, but the matched narrow Harvest pilot still failed the manuscript's evidence gate because schema completeness remained too uneven: the mean effective-LLM fraction stayed at \(0.7311\) and unrepaired fallback averaged \(0.2689\), with missing keys as the dominant failure mode. Accordingly, Harvest LLM evidence is treated only as diagnostic, and the main Study 2 claim remains anchored in the stronger non-LLM search-over-mutations evidence.

The learned-policy extensions remain narrow. In Fishery Commons, the PPO validation is limited to the medium tier and fixed governance conditions. In Harvest Commons, a medium-tier PPO self-play validation supports the \texttt{hybrid} \(>\) \texttt{top\_down\_only} direction, but it still uses fixed governance conditions, shaped rewards, and held-out evaluation in only one tier. These checks are sufficient to show that the leading Fishery and Harvest rankings are not artifacts of one hand-specified policy family, but they are not yet learned-policy invasion benchmarks and they do not replace the main mutation or search-over-mutations evidence.

Hard settings function primarily as stress tests. In fishery, once collapse saturates at 1.0 and survival at 0.0 across conditions, governance separation becomes non-identifiable under floor effects. In Harvest Commons, the harder tiers remain informative, but garden failure itself does not separate conditions in the high-power rows; the main signal comes from patch health, welfare, and local behavioral metrics.

Finally, both substrates remain intentionally stylized. This stylization aids identification, but it limits external realism. Market feedbacks, richer heterogeneity, richer language interaction, and explicit institutional bargaining are natural extensions once the current governance comparisons are fixed.
