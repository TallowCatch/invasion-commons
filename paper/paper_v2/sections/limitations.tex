\section{Limitations}

The two studies answer adjacent questions, not identical ones. Study~1 is a controlled signal-ranking study in Fishery Commons, while Study~2 is a governance-architecture extension in Harvest Commons. That makes the paper intellectually coherent, but it also means Study~2 should not be read as a strict replication of Study~1 in another substrate.

The LLM comparison currently appears only in Study~1. This is enough to show that minimal central governance survives both mutation and live JSON-based strategy generation, but it does not yet establish the same result for Study~2. Harvest Commons still uses structured non-LLM agents with communication and side-payments rather than live language-model control.

Hard settings remain useful mainly as stress evidence. In fishery, once collapse saturates at 1.0 and survival at 0.0 across conditions, governance separation becomes non-identifiable under floor effects. In Harvest Commons, the harder tiers remain informative, but welfare trade-offs become sharper and require more runs if one wants stronger cell-by-cell claims.

Finally, both substrates remain intentionally stylized. That is a strength for identification, but it limits external realism. Market feedbacks, richer heterogeneity, richer language interaction, and explicit institutional bargaining are natural next additions once the current governance comparisons are frozen.
