\section{Methods}

The substrate is a minimal renewable fishery commons with a shared stock, per-agent harvest actions, regeneration, and collapse dynamics. Governance mechanisms modify strategic pressure through monitoring and sanctions rather than only post-hoc accounting, so institutional choices can causally affect ecological outcomes.

Each run is evaluated under three governance settings: none, monitoring, and monitoring with sanctions. Primary contrasts are reported as $\Delta(\texttt{monitoring\_sanctions}-\texttt{none})$. For collapse readability, we also report \texttt{none - monitoring\_sanctions} as ``collapse reduction.''

Two injector sources are matched. The first is \textbf{mutation}, a non-LLM strategy variation baseline. The second is \textbf{llm\_json}, where strategies are generated online by Ollama (\texttt{qwen2.5:3b-instruct}) as structured JSON and then adapted into executable policy specifications.

The core matrix spans tiers (\texttt{easy\_v1}, \texttt{medium\_v1}) and injectors (mutation, llm\_json) with shared settings. Run counts follow the curated cycle outputs, where easy-tier cells have $n\_pairs=3$ and medium-tier high-power cells have $n\_pairs=5$.

Selection and replacement are performed in train conditions, while governance robustness is evaluated on held-out regime packs each generation. This separates adaptation pressure from generalization assessment.

The primary outcome is held-out collapse reduction. Secondary outcomes are held-out mean stock, time-to-collapse, and per-regime survival fraction over generations. A composite governance score averages paired effect sizes ($d_z$) for collapse reduction, stock gain, and survival gain.

Analysis is run-level and CI reporting uses bootstrap mean confidence intervals (B=5000, percentile 2.5/97.5) over paired governance deltas. Gate logic is evaluated from frozen summary artifacts to prevent post-hoc threshold drift.
