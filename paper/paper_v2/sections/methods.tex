\section{Methods}

The substrate is a minimal renewable fishery commons with a shared stock, per-agent harvest actions, regeneration, and collapse dynamics. Governance mechanisms modify strategic pressure through monitoring and sanctions rather than only post-hoc accounting, so institutional choices can causally affect ecological outcomes.

Each run is evaluated under three governance settings: none, monitoring, and monitoring with sanctions. Primary contrasts are reported as the difference between monitoring with sanctions and none. For collapse readability, we also report the same comparison as ``collapse reduction,'' where positive values indicate lower collapse under governance.

Two injector sources are matched. The first is mutation, a non-LLM strategy variation baseline. The second is live JSON-based LLM injection, where strategies are generated online by the Ollama model \texttt{qwen2.5:3b-instruct} and adapted into executable policy specifications.

The core matrix spans the easy\_v1 and medium\_v1 tiers and both injector types, with shared settings. Run counts follow the curated cycle outputs, where easy-tier cells use three paired runs and medium-tier high-power cells use five paired runs.

Selection and replacement are performed in train conditions, while governance robustness is evaluated on held-out regime packs each generation. This separates adaptation pressure from generalization assessment.

The primary outcome is held-out collapse reduction. Secondary outcomes are held-out mean stock, time-to-collapse, and per-regime survival fraction over generations. A composite governance score averages paired effect sizes for collapse reduction, stock gain, and survival gain.

Analysis is run-level and confidence intervals are estimated with a bootstrap mean procedure using 5{,}000 resamples and percentile bounds at 2.5 and 97.5. Gate logic is evaluated from frozen summary artifacts to prevent post-hoc threshold drift.

Methods and run metadata are frozen in curated outputs within the repository, including the methods summary, gate outcomes, and main delta tables used to generate the reported statistics.
