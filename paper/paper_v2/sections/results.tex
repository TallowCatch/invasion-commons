\section{Results}

Under matched settings with five paired high-power runs, monitoring with sanctions reduced held-out collapse relative to none for both injector types. For mutation, collapse reduction was $0.109896$ with 95\% CI $[0.031042, 0.189583]$. For live JSON-based LLM injection, collapse reduction was $0.083542$ with 95\% CI $[0.015417, 0.150938]$. Both confidence-interval lower bounds are positive, supporting the medium-tier robustness claim for governance under invasion pressure.

Monitoring with sanctions also improved supporting robustness indicators relative to none. Mean held-out stock increased by $23.352178$ for mutation with 95\% CI $[15.873344, 31.697602]$, and by $22.402775$ for live JSON-based LLM injection with 95\% CI $[18.453585, 27.746404]$. Per-regime survival over generations improved by $0.109896$ for mutation with 95\% CI $[0.031042, 0.189583]$, and by $0.083542$ for live JSON-based LLM injection with 95\% CI $[0.015417, 0.151667]$. Time to collapse increased by $1.2$ generations for mutation with 95\% CI $[0.4, 2.2]$, and by $1.8$ generations for live JSON-based LLM injection with 95\% CI $[1.0, 3.0]$.

From the frozen gate file, the cycle-level criteria evaluate as follows: the primary medium reduction gate is PASS, the strong confidence-interval lower-bound gate is PASS, and the medium saturation check is FALSE.

Easy-tier runs used three paired runs and show wider uncertainty, especially for live JSON-based LLM injection. For collapse reduction, mutation remains positive at $0.170139$ with 95\% CI $[0.068750, 0.279167]$, while live JSON-based LLM injection crosses zero at $0.163542$ with 95\% CI $[-0.061458, 0.411979]$. This is consistent with treating medium-tier high-power evidence as the decision-critical result.
