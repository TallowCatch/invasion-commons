\section{Results}

Under matched settings with high-power reruns ($n\_pairs=5$), monitoring+sanctions reduced held-out collapse relative to none for both injector types. For mutation, collapse reduction was $0.109896$ (95\% CI $[0.031042, 0.189583]$). For live llm\_json injection, collapse reduction was $0.083542$ (95\% CI $[0.015417, 0.150938]$). Both confidence-interval lower bounds are positive, supporting the medium-tier robustness claim for governance under invasion pressure.

Monitoring+sanctions also improved supporting robustness indicators versus none. Mean held-out stock increased by $23.352178$ for mutation (95\% CI $[15.873344, 31.697602]$) and by $22.402775$ for llm\_json (95\% CI $[18.453585, 27.746404]$). Per-regime survival over generations improved by $0.109896$ for mutation (95\% CI $[0.031042, 0.189583]$) and by $0.083542$ for llm\_json (95\% CI $[0.015417, 0.151667]$). Time to collapse increased by $1.2$ generations for mutation (95\% CI $[0.4, 2.2]$) and by $1.8$ generations for llm\_json (95\% CI $[1.0, 3.0]$).

From the frozen gate file, the cycle-level criteria evaluate as follows: the primary medium reduction gate is \textbf{PASS}, the strong CI lower-bound gate is \textbf{PASS}, and the medium saturation check ($>0.95$ collapse for all conditions) is \textbf{FALSE}.

Easy-tier runs used $n\_pairs=3$ and show wider uncertainty, especially for llm\_json. For collapse reduction, mutation remains positive ($0.170139$, 95\% CI $[0.068750, 0.279167]$), while llm\_json crosses zero ($0.163542$, 95\% CI $[-0.061458, 0.411979]$). This is consistent with treating medium-tier high-power evidence as the decision-critical result.
