\begin{abstract}
We study cooperation robustness in a minimal sequential fishery commons under adversarial strategy injection. Rather than asking whether cooperation emerges in a fixed population, we evaluate whether governance signals preserve cooperation under repeated strategy turnover and held-out regime shifts. The paper\_v1 cycle uses a matched matrix across two regime tiers (easy\_v1, medium\_v1), two injector types (mutation and live llm\_json via Ollama), and three governance conditions (none, monitoring, monitoring+sanctions). In medium\_v1 high-power reruns, monitoring+sanctions reduces held-out collapse relative to none for both injectors with positive confidence bounds, supporting invasion-stability gains from minimal governance. Hard-tier results are treated as appendix-only stress evidence due to saturation.
\end{abstract}
